15.05 Elk Hunting Permits/Tags.

(1) No member shall hunt elk pursuant to this chapter without possessing a valid antlerless elk hunting permit or a valid bull elk hunting permit approved by the Tribe. Such a permit may be the member's tribal identification card required by Section 3.02(2).

(2)(a) No member shall hunt elk without possessing a valid carcass tag, except as provided in Section 15.11 [Group Elk Hunting].

(b) The tribal conservation department shall issue no more than one (1) carcass tag to a member, at one time.

(3) (a) No member shall hunt antlerless elk without a valid antlerless elk hunting permit bearing the number of the member's carcass tag and valid for the date on which the member is hunting, except as provided in Section 15.11 [Group Elk Hunting].

(b) No member shall hunt bull elk without a valid bull elk hunting permit bearing the number of the member's carcass tag and valid for the date on which the member is hunting, except as provided in Section 15.11 [Group Elk Hunting].

(4) No antlerless elk hunting permits or bull elk hunting permits shall be considered valid for any elk management unit, or portion of any elk management unit:

(a) which is shown to be closed on the permit; or

(b) which has been deactivated by the tribe's permitting system.

(5) No member shall move or field dress an elk before affixing to it a valid carcass tag.

(6) Except as otherwise provided in this ordinance, antlerless elk permits or bull elk permits shall

be valid for the open season specified in Section 15.07.

(7) The number, and intertribal distribution, of antlerless elk permits or bull elk permits, which may be issued at any one time for an elk management zone during the open season shall be determined in accordance with the procedures and methods approved pursuant to the Chippewa Intertribal Agreement Concerning Resource Management and Regulation of Off-Reservation Treaty Rights in the Ceded Territory, and the Voigt Intertribal Task Force Protocol Noodamashkoozwe (Elk Harvest).
