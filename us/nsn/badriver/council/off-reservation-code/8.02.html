8.02 Definitions.

For the purpose of this chapter, the following terms shall be construed to apply as follows:

(1) "Body Gripping Trap" means a trap that is designed to allow the animal's head, neck or torso to enter the trap opening and be held by compression of the jaws around the head, neck or torso when the trap is sprung and which is not a cage trap, enclosed trigger trap, snare, restraining snare or jawed trap.

(2) "Bow" shall have the meaning prescribed in Section 6.01(2) [Definitions].

(3) "Box Trap" means a trap that is not used as a water set or in conjunction with a jawed trap, an enclosed trigger trap or body gripping trap, and that is designed to allow an animal to enter the trap enclosure, be captured and remain alive inside the cage or box type enclosure until it can be released unharmed or reduced to possession by the operator of the trap.

(4) "Colony Trap" means while trapping to use an enclosure device designed to allow the capture of one or more fur-bearing animals in a single setting as a drowning set. Entrance to the device is gained by one or more one-way entrances with overall dimensions of the trap not to exceed 6 ¼" (six and one quarter inches) in width, 6 ¼" (six and one quarter inches) in height and 36" (thirty-six inches) in length.

(5) "Crossbow" shall have the meaning prescribed in Section 6.01(3) [Definitions].

(6) "Dry Land Set" means any trap that is set or staked in such a manner as to prevent the trapped animal from reaching water that is not frozen.

(7) "Enclosed Trigger Trap" means any trap with a pull-activated trigger, inside an enclosure, and recessed 1 ¼ inches or more, from an opening that is no greater than 1 ¾ inches in diameter.

(8) "Firearm" shall have the meaning prescribed in Section 6.01(4) [Definitions].

(9) "Fur Bearing Animals" means beaver, bobcat, cottontail rabbit, coyote, fisher, fox, squirrel, grey fox, grey squirrel, mink, muskrat, otter, raccoon, red fox, red squirrel, snowshoe hare, and any mammalian variety of unprotected species as defined herein.

(10) "Hunt" or "Hunting" shall have the meaning prescribed in Section 2.01(8) [Definitions] but shall not include trapping for the purposes of this chapter.

(11) "Jawed Trap" means a trap designed to catch any animal by the foot, but does not include enclosed trigger traps, body gripping traps or snares.

(12) "Restraining Snare" means a wire noose with a relaxing lock used for restraining furbearing animals which does not allow the device or trapped animal to reach unfrozen water.

(13) "Small Game" means beaver, bobcat, bobwhite quail, cottontail rabbit, coyote, fisher, fox squirrel, grey fox, grey squirrel, Hungarian partridge (grey partridge), mink, muskrat, otter, pheasant, raccoon, red fox, red squirrel, ruffed grouse, sharp-tailed grouse, snowshoe hare, unprotected species.

(14) "Snare" means a noose with a non-relaxing lock used for catching furbearing animals.

(15) "Trap", when used as a noun, shall mean a body-gripping trap, colony trap, enclosed trigger trap, box trap, jawed trap, snare or restraining snare. When the word "trap" is used as a verb, it shall have the same meaning as the word "trapping" as defined herein.

(16) "Trapping" includes the taking or possessing of, or attempting to take or possess, any wild animal by means of setting or operating any device, mechanism or contraption that is designed, built or made to close upon, hold fast, or otherwise capture a wild animal or animals.

(17) "Unprotected Species" means:

(a) Opossum, skunk, weasel, woodchuck;

(b) Starlings, English sparrows, coturnix quail, chukar partridge and rock doves;

(c) All other unprotected species as defined in Section 2.01(18) [Definitions].

(18) "Water Set" means any trap which is set or staked in such a manner as to permit the trap or trapped animal to reach unfrozen water.
