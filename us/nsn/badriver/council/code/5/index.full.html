Title 5 Gaming

Chapter 5.01 Gaming Control

5.01.010 Findings

The Tribal Council finds that gaming revenues are essential to the provision of tribal governmental services, that direct tribal control through the central tribal administration is essential to the rational utilization of tribal funds generated by tribally-operated gaming, that tribal gaming revenues must be fully integrated into the tribe's annual financial planning; and that tribal governmental spending priorities must take precedence over individual or organizational funding requests in the utilization of gaming receipts.

Subchapter B Electronic Bingo

5.01.090 General

Under 25 U.S.C. § 2703(7) of the IGRA, Class II games may utilize electronic, computer or other technologic aids. At the same time, IGRA strictly prohibits the use of electronic or electromechanical facsimiles of any game of chance without a tribal-state compact or procedures issued by the Secretary of the Interior. Any Gaming System that is determined by the Tribal Gaming Commission not to be Class II may only be operated at the Tribal Gaming Facility pursuant to the terms of a tribal-state compact or Class III gaming procedures.

5.01.100 Definitions

For the purposes of this subchapter, the following definitions shall be used. Words in the present tense include the future; the singular includes the plural; and the plural includes the singular. The word "shall" is mandatory and the word "may" is permissive.

Electronic Bingo (Class II)

(a) Definitions

(1) "Bingo" means the game of chance set forth in the IGRA at 25 U.S.C. § 2703(7)(A)(i), (whether or not electronic, computer, or other technologic aids are used in connection therewith):

(A) Which is played for Prizes, including monetary Prizes, with Cards bearing numbers or other designations;

(B) In which the holder of the Card covers such numbers or designations when objects, similarly numbered or designated, are drawn or electronically determined; and

(C) In which the game is won by the first person covering a previously designated arrangement of numbers or designations on such Cards.

(2) "Card" means an array of numbers or other designations, presented on paper and/or in electronic form, that is used by a player as a basis to Cover such numbers or other designations drawn or electronically determined in a Bingo game.

(3) "Claim" means a player's declaration of the right to a Prize under the Rules of Play.

(4) "Classification Determination" means a formal determination by the Tribal Gaming Commission that a Gaming System is Class II under this Regulation.

(5) "Consideration" means the player's payment or credit to participate or to continue participating in a game.

(6) "Council" or "Tribal Council" means the seven (7) member committee composed of elected tribal officials authorized to represent the Tribe pursuant to the Tribe's Constitution.

(7) "Cover" means the process of matching or daubing the numbers or other designations on a Card that correspond to numbers or other designations from a Draw.

(8) "Draw" means the physical or electronic selection of numbers or other designations from the set of numbers or other designations used to determine winners in a Bingo game.

(9) "Electronic, Computer or Other Technologic Aid"" has the meaning set forth in Section 5.01.130(a)(2) of this Regulation.

(10) "Electronic or Electromechanical Facsimile" has the meaning set forth in Section 5.01.130(a)(3) of this Regulation.

(11) "Game Winning Card" means a Card on which a Game Winning Pattern has been Covered. There may be more than one Game Winning Card in any Bingo game.

(12) "Game Winning Pattern" means a specific array of numbers or other designations on a Card that, when Covered on the Card(s), entitles the player(s) to receive the Game Winning Prize.

(13) "Tribal Gaming Commission" means a Tribally designated entity responsible for Gaming regulation.

(14) "Gaming System" means all components, including electronic, computer, or other technologic equipment, that function together to aid the play of one or more games of Bingo including any Player Interfaces.

(15) "NIGC" means the National Indian Gaming Commission.

(16) "NIGC Technical Standards" means the Technical Standards for Electronic, Computer, or Other Technologic Aids Used in the Play of Class II Games, 25 C.F.R. Part 547.

(17) "Pattern" means, in the case of a Game Winning Pattern, an array of two or more numbers or other designations on a Card. A Pattern for a Prize Winning Pattern must contain at least one number or other designation on a Card.

(18) "Player Interface" means any component or components of a Gaming System, including an electronic or technologic aid such as terminals, player stations, handhelds, fixed units, etc. that directly enables player interaction in a game of Bingo.

(19) "Prize" means anything of material value, including money, which is awarded to a player under the Rules of Play.

(20) "Prize Pool" means the cumulative amount of contributions paid by participants in one of more games of Bingo, which is used to fund prizes specified in the Rules of Play.

(21) "Prize Winning Card" means a Card on which a Prize Winning Pattern has been Covered, entitling a player to Claim an interim or consolation Prize under the Rules of Play. There may be more than one Prize Winning Card in any Bingo game.

(22) "Prize Winning Pattern" means a specific array of numbers or other designations that, when Covered on a Card(s), entitles the player(s) to receive an interim or consolation Prize.

(23) "Random Number Generator (RNG)" means an electronic or mechanical method for determining numbers or other designations in a game of chance that must meet the applicable standards for randomness set forth in the NIGC Technical Standards or established by the Tribal Gaming Commission.

(24) "Rules of Play" means the specific rules controlling the play of the subject game of Bingo.

(25) "Server" means a computer which controls one or more applications or environments within a Gaming System.

(26) "Tribal Gaming Facility" means the Casino, which is located on the Indian lands of the Tribe operated pursuant to the Tribe's Gaming Ordinance.

(27) "Tribal Gaming Facility Manager (TGFM)" means the Casino Gaming Manager who operates the gaming facility for the Tribe.

5.01.110 Classification Procedure

(a) Application Process

(1) Only a duly authorized representative of the GM may apply for a Classification Determination under this Regulation.

(2) The GM may apply for a Classification Determination at any time, but must do so before offering a new Gaming System for play under the following circumstances:

(A The GM wishes to install a new Gaming System that is not the subject of an existing Classification Determination and is not otherwise permitted as a Class III game; or

(B) The GM wishes to introduce a material modification to a previously approved Gaming System, including a Gaming system approved before the effective date of this Regulation.

(C) Although not required, the GM also may apply for a Classification Determination under this Regulation for a Gaming System approved by the Tribal Gaming Commission before the effective date of this Regulation.

(b) Application Submission Requirements

(1) To obtain a Classification Determination, the GM must submit an application to the Tribal Gaming Commission, which consists of the following documentation and information:

(A) Request for a Classification Determination;

(B) Certification by the vendor of the Gaming System that the Gaming System to be placed in the Tribal Gaming Facility:

(i) Is within the criteria for a Class II game of Bingo under IGRA and this Regulation;

(ii) Complies in all respects with the specifications and standards set forth in the application; and

(iii) Complies with any applicable regulations concerning such Gaming System.

(C) A legal opinion that concludes that the Gaming System is a Class II game of Bingo under the IGRA played using a permitted electronic, computer or other technologic aids;

(D) GM's statement that, to the best of its knowledge and belief, the subject Gaming System meets the standards in these Regulations; [optional]

(E) Designation of a point of contact authorized to provide additional information if required;

(F) Designation from the vendor of whether and where the Gaming System is already in play;

(G) Complete written description from the vendor of the Gaming System, including the operational characteristics and Rules of Play;

(H) Complete description of the method used for accepting Consideration from players, paying or distributing Prizes to winners, determining, retaining or paying any amounts to the operator or vendor from the Consideration or any other source, and the awarding or funding of Prize Pools;

(I) Copy of any of the vendor's sales or promotional literature for the Gaming System; [Optional]

(J) For Gaming Systems already in play at the Tribal Gaming Facility, a complete list of the serial numbers or other identifiers of each Player Interface or other device used in the play of the Gaming System;

(K) Report of laboratory test(s), which must be addressed to the Tribal Gaming Commission, which were conducted that validate that the Gaming System meets the requirements of Sections B(1)(b)(ii) and (iii) of this Article, as well as the requirements of the NIGC Technical Standards found at 25 C.F.R. Part 547;

(L) Copies of any court or administrative decisions or other materials, supplied by the Gaming System vendor, which address the classification of the proposed Gaming System, if any;

(c) Submission of Additional Information

Upon request of the Tribal Gaming Commission, the GM may be required to provide any further information or clarification the Tribal Gaming Commission deems necessary to make a Classification Determination.

5.01.130 Classification Criteria

(a) Factors to be Considered in Making Classification Determination

(1) The critical factors in determining whether a Gaming System is Class II are:

(A) Whether the underlying game is a Class II game of Bingo; and

(B) Whether the electronic equipment utilized in connection with the game:

(i) Is an electronic, computer or other technologic aid, which is permitted within Class II; or

(ii) Causes the game to be an electromechanical facsimile of a game of chance.

(2) An electronic, computer or other technologic aid:

(A) Is defined as electronic, computer, or other technologic equipment used in connection with a game of Bingo that:

(i) Assists a player or the playing of a game;

(ii) Is not an electromechanical facsimile; and

(iii) Is operated in accordance with applicable federal communications law.

(B) Includes, but is not limited to, electronic, computer, or other technologic equipment used in connection with a Bingo game that:

(i) Broadens participation in the game;

(ii) Facilitates communication between and among gaming sites; or

(iii) Requires a player to play a game with or against other players rather than with or against a machine.

(C) Examples include, but are not limited to, telephones, cables, televisions, screens, satellites, bingo blowers, electronic player stations and electronic Cards.

(3) An electromechanical facsimile is a game played in an electronic or electromechanical format that replicates a game of chance by incorporating all of the characteristics of the game, except when for a Class II game such as Bingo, the electronic or electromechanical format broadens participation by allowing multiple players to play with or against each other rather than with or against a machine.

(b) Determination that Gaming System is Class II

(1) Bingo, as played using a Gaming System, will be determined to be a Class II game if it possesses the attributes of Bingo as set forth in the definition of Bingo in this Regulation.

(2) The Tribal Gaming Commission has determined that the following attributes are required by the definition of Bingo:

(A) The underlying game consists of:

(i) A set quantity of Draw numbers or other designations (hereinafter referred to as "Symbols");

(ii) A set number of Cards bearing an array or layout of numbers or other designations available for play in a particular game of Bingo;

(iii) Predetermined Game Winning Pattern(s); and

(iv) Prizes, including a Game Winning Prize and optional interim and consolation Prizes, as defined under the Rules of Play.

(B) The Draw, if conducted using a Random Number Generator, must meet applicable standards for ensuring randomness as set forth in the NIGC Technical Standards or established by the Tribal Gaming Commission.

(C) Actual Prize values must be defined before a Prize can be awarded, but need not be defined before the start of a game.

(D) A game must be played by a minimum of two players.

(3) The Tribal Gaming Commission has determined that the following attributes are consistent with, but not required by, the definition of Bingo:

(A) The number of Symbols in a Draw is not fixed; it may be varied from game to game, in accordance with the Rules of Play. Symbols may be displayed, without limitation, as numbers, letters, icons, any other graphic or other enhancement, or any combination thereof.

(B) A game may include any number of Cards. The Card may be multi-dimensional. Each Card must have an array or layout of symbols and contain at least two of the Draw symbols. Game Winning Patterns and Prize Winning Patterns can be any configuration of matched symbols on the player's Card. Interim and consolation Prizes also can be awarded for the absence of matched symbols.

(C) When playing Bingo, a Draw of a fixed number of Symbols may take place before Cards are purchased by, or distributed to, some or all of the players. Players then compete in a given game by purchasing Cards and then revealing the designations on purchased Cards, so that the pre-drawn Symbols may be Covered. Subsequent Draws may take place to assure that a Game Winning Pattern is achieved.

(D) Multiple Prizes may be specified in a game based on, but not necessarily limited to, different Patterns or the number of drawn Symbols required to achieve a specified Pattern. There may be multiple winners, on multiple Cards. Prize levels can also vary for the same Pattern as a function of the player's Consideration for the Card and/or how far the Draw has progressed. Prizes may include interim or consolation Prizes where the Prize is based on the specific order in which the numbers are selected. Progressive Prizes may also be awarded based on specified criteria reflecting sequence or specific characteristics of the Symbols Covered by the player.

(E) A player may play more than one Card in a game of Bingo.

(4) In determining whether Bingo, as played using a Gaming System, is a Class II game, the Tribal Gaming Commission should not apply any requirements that would:

(A) Limit the use of electronic aids to play Bingo, display Cards, Cover Symbols, identify Game or Prize Winning Patterns, or Claim Prizes;

(B) Limit the speed of play, Card configuration, or number of Draws in a game;

(C) Limit how or when Cards are distributed to players;

(D) Limit the sequence of a Draw;

(E) Limit how a player Claims a Prize;

(F) Limit the type of entertaining display on the Player Interface; or

(G) Limit the maximum number of players.

5.01.140 Classification Decisions

(a) Issuance of a Written Classification Determination

If the Tribal Gaming Commission determines that the Gaming System meets the criteria in Article 6 above, it shall issue a written Classification Determination and shall provide copies of such Classification Determination to the Tribal Council and the GM.

(b) Issuance of an Unfavorable Determination

If the Tribal Gaming Commission determines that the Gaming System does not meet one or more of the criteria in Article 6 above, it shall issue a written decision specifically addressing the reasons for its determination and shall provide copies of such decision to the Tribal Council and the GM.

(c) Finality of the Decision

If the Tribal Gaming Commission determines that a Gaming System is not Class II for any reason, the decision of the Tribal Gaming Commission will be final and binding. The Tribal Gaming Commission may, in its discretion, allow a request for reconsideration upon a showing of new factual evidence or legal support.

5.01.150 Installation of Gaming Systems

(a) Confirmation of Specifications

The Tribal Gaming Commission requires the manufacturer or distributor of Gaming Systems intended for use in this jurisdiction to certify, in writing, that prior to delivery to the Tribe, each Gaming System:

(1) Conforms precisely to the exact specifications of the system prototype tested and approved by the test laboratory.

(2) Can be tested using one or more unique signatures, checksum values or similar technology for the operating programs used with the electronic computer, or other technologic aid.

(3) Operates and plays in accordance with the NIGC Technical Standards, this Regulation and all other applicable Tribal Gaming Commission regulations.

(b) Integrity of Seals

Upon delivery of a Gaming System to the Tribal Gaming Facility, no one will break the seal of any delivery container, nor will any system be removed from the shipping container without the physical presence and authorization of a Tribal Gaming Commission official.

(c) Use of Approved Locks

The GM shall replace Player Interface locks with locks approved for use by the Tribal Gaming Commission.

(d) System Modifications

The GM shall develop policies and procedures to be approved by the Tribal Gaming Commission for installing, accessing, maintaining, repairing, and configuring systems in the same manner as set forth in Tribal Gaming Commission regulations.

(e) Delivery of Gaming Systems

At least ten (10) calendar days prior to shipping and delivery of a Gaming System, each manufacturer or distributor shall report in writing to the Tribal Gaming Commission the following information for each Player Interface to be used as part of the Gaming System:

(1) The type of Player Interface (i.e. hand-held or fixed-base).

(2) The Player Interface serial number.

(3) The manufacturer of the Player Interface.

(4) The vendor from whom the Player Interface was acquired, the means by which the Player Interface will be transported into the state, and the name and street address of any common carrier or other person transporting the Player Interface.

(5) The date of shipping and anticipated delivery date.

(f) Notice of New or Upgraded Software

The Tribal Gaming Commission shall be notified by the GM of any subsequent purchases of any new or upgraded program software within ten (10) calendar days.

5.04.020 METHOD OF PLAY

(a) Types of Raffles. There shall be two types of raffles which may be conducted:

(1) Raffles in which tickets are sold to individuals, placed in a container and drawn to determine the winner, and

(2) Raffles in which a fixed number of tickets, some which indicate the winners, are sold and drawn by the purchaser.

(b) Tickets. There shall be three types of tickets which shall be used for the raffles in subsection (a):

(1) For raffles described in (a)(1) tickets shall be identical in form and numbered sequentially if the raffle tickets are only sold on the same day or night of the occasion and drawing of the winner.

(2) For raffles described in (a)(1) and if the raffle tickets are sold over a period of time before the day or night of the drawing of the winner, the tickets shall be identical in form, contain the name and address of the Tribe or sponsoring organization, the price of the ticket, the date and place of the drawing and a place for the purchaser's name and address. Sale of these raffle tickets off premises shall be permitted.

(3) For raffles describe in (b)(2) raffle tickets shall be purchased in lots, with a fixed number of tickets and winning tickets in each lot, shall be of similar outward appearance and shall have concealed within the indices of winning what may be revealed by manipulation of the ticket by the purchasers after the purchase.

(c) Play of Raffles:

(1) The Tribal Bingo Manager shall determine the type of raffle and the prize or prizes for each raffle, which shall be clearly described and available to raffle ticket purchasers at the time of purchase. For raffles described in Section (a)(1), the odds per lot of tickets shall also be available.

(2) Each raffle shall be conducted in such manner that the results are random and each person purchasing a ticket is afforded an equal opportunity to win, and all equipment and supplies used in the conduct of each raffle shall be designed and used in such a manner as to secure such a result.

(3) Winners shall be determined according to the type of raffle and the rules of each raffle set by the Tribal Bingo Manager. The purchaser of a ticket need not be present at the drawing to win a prize.

(4) All raffle drawings shall be held in public.

(5) All prizes shall be awarded.

5.06.020 Class III Gaming - Rules of Play

(a) No person under 21 years of age may play or be permitted to play. If any person below the age of 21 years plays and otherwise qualifies for a prize or winnings, the prize or winning shall not be paid, and the estimated amount wagered during the course of the game shall be returned to the minor.

(c) The chief management official of the Class III gaming operation shall cause to be posted at places where notice is likely that an announcement is most visible that persons under the age of 21 may not participate in gaming.

(d) No person who is visibly intoxicated may play any Class III game.

(e) No credit shall be extended by the Tribe or any of its agents or employees for the conduct of gaming. No person shall offer any other person credit for a fee, however calculated for the conduct of gaming, except that an automatic teller machine that accepts bank cards or credit cards may be installed upon Class III gaming premises.

(f) No alcoholic beverages may be sold at the Class III facility for the purpose of off-premises consumption.

(g) Electronic games of chance shall be installed and operated pursuant to section XV of the current Compact entered into by the Tribe and the State of Wisconsin

(h) Blackjack shall be conducted pursuant to Section XVI of the current Compact entered into by the Tribe and the State of Wisconsin.

(i) Pulltab or break open ticket games shall be conducted pursuant to the most recent published standards of the North American Gaming Regulators Association.

The decision of the management official shall be final. If the dispute involves the chief management official on the premises, it shall be resolved within five working days by the tribal chairman or, in his absence, the vice-chairman, after hearing from the aggrieved player and the involved management official. (2) The Tribe's personnel director shall forward the report referred to in subsection (e) of this section to the National Indian Gaming Commission within 60 days after an employee begins work or within 50 days of the approval of this ordinance by the Chairman of the National Indian Gaming Commission.

(3) The gaming operation shall not employ as a key employee or primary management official, a person who does not receive a license within 90 days of hiring.

(e) Report to the National Indian Gaming Commission

(1) Pursuant to the procedures set out in subsection (d) of this section, the Tribe shall prepare and forward to the National Indian Gaming Commission an investigative report on each background investigation. An investigative report shall include all of the following:

(A) Steps taken in conducting a background investigation;

(B) Results obtained;

(C) Conclusions reached; and

(D) The bases for those conclusions.

(2) The Tribe's personnel director shall submit, with the report, a copy of the eligibility determination made under subsection (c) of this section.

(3) If a license is not issued to an applicant, the Tribe shall:

(A) Notify the National Indian Gaming Commission; and

(B) Forward copies of its eligibility determination and investigation report (if any) to the National Indian Gaming Commission for inclusion in the Indian Gaming Individuals Records System.

(4) With respect to key employees and primary management officials, the Tribe shall retain applications for employment and reports (if any) of background investigations for inspection by the Chairman of the National Indian Gaming Commission or his or her designee for no less than three (3) years from the date of termination of employment.

(f) Granting A Gaming License

(1) The Tribal Council shall issue all gaming licenses.

(2) If, within a thirty (30) day period after the National Indian Gaming Commission receives a report, the National Indian Gaming Commission notifies the tribe that it has no objection to the issuance of a license pursuant to a license application filed by a key employee or a primary management official for whom the tribe has provided an application and investigative report to the National Indian Gaming Commission, the Tribe's personnel director may recommend that the Tribal Council issue a license to such applicant.

(3) The Tribe shall respond to a request for additional information from the Chairman of the National Indian Gaming Commission concerning a key employee or a primary management official who is the subject of a report. Such a request shall suspend the 30-day period under paragraph (f)(2) of this section until the Chairman of the National Indian Gaming Commission receives the additional information.

(4) If, within the thirty (30) day period described above the National Indian Gaming Commission provides the Tribe with a statement itemizing objections to the issuance of a license to a key employee or to a primary management official for whom the tribe has provided an application, the Tribe shall reconsider the application taking into account the objections itemized by the National Indian Gaming Commission. The Tribe's personnel director shall supply to the Tribal Council the Native Indian Gaming Commission statement, prior to council action on the license. The Tribal Council shall make the final decision whether to issue a license to such applicant.

(h) License Suspension

(1) If, after the issuance of a gaming license, the Tribe receives from the National Indian Gaming Commission reliable information indicating that a key employee or a primary management official is not eligible for employment under subsection (c) above, the Tribe's personnel director shall suspend such license and shall notify in writing the licensee of the suspension and the proposed revocation.

(2) The Tribe's personnel director shall notify the licensee of a time and a place for a hearing on the proposed revocation of a license, which shall be conducted before the Tribal Council under rules as it may prescribe.

(3) After a revocation hearing, the Tribal Council shall decide to revoke or to reinstate a gaming license. The decision of the Tribal Council shall be final. The Tribe shall notify the National Indian Gaming Commission of is decision.
